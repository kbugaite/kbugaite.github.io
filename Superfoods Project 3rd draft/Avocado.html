Avocado

The avocado is a medium-sized, evergreen tree in the laurel family, native to the Americas. It is widely cultivated for its large, fleshy fruit. The tree likely originated in the highland regions of south-central Mexico to Guatemala.
Avocados contain high levels of healthy, beneficial fats, which can help a person feel fuller between meals.
Also known as an alligator pear or butter fruit, avocados are actually a type of berry.
Avocados are a source of vitamins C, E, K, and B6, as well as riboflavin, niacin, folate, pantothenic acid, magnesium, and potassium. They also provide lutein, beta carotene, and omega-3 fatty acids.
